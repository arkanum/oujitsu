What is Jitsu?

Jitsu is a martial art. Jiu Jitsu (meaning 'gentle art') originated and developed in medieval Japan. We still use Japanese names and terminology for many of our techniques. The style of Jiu Jitsu taught by the Jitsu Foundation is called Shorinji Kan Jiu Jitsu.

Jitsu provides the practitioner with a highly effective, practical system of self-defence. The core of the art comprises a system of throws, joint locks and strikes. It is based on the principal of using an aggressor's body weight and momentum to their own disadvantage.

Jitsu skills can be used to counter aggressive situations ranging from harassment to armed physical assault, irrespective of the defender's physique or gender. In training, we don't tend to spar. Because Jitsu is a defensive martial art we tend to train in pairs or groups taking turns to attack and defend. Jitsu is taught in a harmonious environment, free from macho posturing. The club aims to improve the skills of all its members.

Jitsu is a progressive martial art that offers a means of personal and physical development, as well as an effective system of self-defence. Regular training can help to improve strength, fitness, flexibility, co-ordination and posture. Moreover, most people find over time that Jitsu improves their self-esteem and self-confidence. Advanced training levels are highly challenging and foster exceptional levels of physical and mental control. Jitsu training is also great fun and a fantastic way to meet new friends. Oxford University Jitsu Club offers a vibrant social life out of club training, with events organised throughout the year.

Oxford University Jitsu Club is affiliated to The Jiu Jitsu Foundation - the largest single-style Jiu Jitsu organisation in Britain. The Jiu Jitsu Foundation has over 3000 members training in more than one hundred clubs.

About the club

Oxford University Jitsu Club was established 15 years ago and has over 50 licenced members - mostly university students but with a very healthy mix of non-students.

The club has an excellent track record for producing high quality jitsuka, frequently winning podium places at national level competitions. We're also the current holder of the Oxford vs Cambridge Jitsu Varsity Shield.
